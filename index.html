Project Technical Basis

Amora Ventures focuses on the implementation of Double Contact Double Absorption (DCDA) technology. This design ensures a 99.7% conversion rate of SO₂ into SO₃, significantly reducing sulfur emissions while maximizing the production of 98% industrial-grade Sulfuric Acid.

Process Engineering Detail

Stage 1: Sulfur Combustion (F-101)

Pure molten sulfur is oxidized in a furnace using dehumidified air to produce Sulfur Dioxide gas. The process temperature is maintained between 900°C - 1100°C.

S(l) + O₂(g) → SO₂(g) | ΔH = -297 kJ/mol

Stage 2: Catalytic Conversion (R-201)

SO₂ is converted into SO₃ through four catalyst beds containing 120 tonnes of Vanadium Pentoxide (V₂O₅).

2SO₂(g) + O₂(g) ⇌ 2SO₃(g) | V₂O₅ Catalyst

Operating Temperature: 420°C - 450°C | Replacement Cycle: 4 Years.

Stage 3: Absorption & Hydration

SO₃ gas is absorbed into concentrated acid to form Oleum, which is then safely diluted with water to reach 98% concentration.

SO₃(g) + H₂SO₄(l) → H₂S₂O₇(l)
H₂S₂O₇(l) + H₂O(l) → 2H₂SO₄(l)
